July 3, 2013: Discharge Day

Jul 3rd, 2013

When we woke in the morning, Stephanie told us that the maternity floor was filling up, and she’d need to move us to a little out-of-the-way room on the labor and delivery floor. So we packed up our things and followed her to our new room. The day nurse didn’t check in on us nearly as often as Stephanie had the night before — probably because we were tucked out of the way on a different floor than all the other new mommies and babies. Given that we weren’t getting nearly as much attention as we had overnight — and that the next day was a holiday — we felt strongly that we wanted to be discharged as soon as possible.

We had to be a little annoying to pull it off, but once Oliver got his 24-hour checkup — and we got to sit in on his circumcision and hearing test — we were able to be discharged and head back to “hotel-home.” The doctor at the hospital was quite reluctant to discharge us, and warned us that the second night would be much harder than the first. It turned out that the second night was just about as easy as the first — and probably easier, because we had gotten to know Oliver a bit better, and because we could be much more independent in the hotel than we’d been able to be in the hospital. Oliver continued to be a little angel. He still wasn’t very active, but he was much more alert than his first day. Our bedroom at the hotel was small, and we didn’t feel comfortable sleeping with him in the bed, so he spent his first night out of the hospital sleeping in a travel bassinet on the floor of the hotel bedroom closet. We finally got out of bed at 10:00 the next morning, surprised at how incredibly well-rested we were.

Welcome Oliver!

Jul 2nd, 2013

Oliver Damien Murphey was born at 2:08 this afternoon! His birth mom did great, enduring an induction, and when that didn’t work, an emergency c-section. We were able to go to the hospital to meet him a little after 6:00. When we got there, Oliver was sleeping peacefully in his birth grandma’s arms. Together, she and I admired his perfect little ears, his sweet little mouth, his beautiful little nose. I held him, and then Rebecca held him. We were so excited to finally meet him!

The hospital staff were very nice, and found us a room on the maternity floor where we could care for Oliver overnight. Our nurse, Stephanie, showed me how to breastfeed him. Because I don’t have a full milk supply, she also helped us use a supplemental nurser with the breast milk (colostrum, actually) that we’d bought from the milk bank a few days before.

Rebecca and I crammed into the tiny hospital bed together, and Oliver slept in his hospital crib right next to our bed. The nurse told us to wake him every three hours to feed him, so three hours after his first feeding, we tried and tried to wake him, but he was way too sleepy. We called Stephanie in and she tried unsuccessfully to wake him too, and then told us that it was all right to wait another hour or so to feed him. We did, and by then he was ready.

Although Oliver only opened his eyes once or twice overnight, we were amazed by how good his head control was. When we laid him face-down on one of our chests, he often lifted up his head.

Once during the night, the nurse came by because Oliver’s monitoring device had fallen off his leg; this completely shut down all the elevators in that part of the hospital to prevent any newborn babies from being snatched. We soon found the device and reattached it to his leg.

Oliver woke and cried a few times in the night, and we were able to calm him fairly easily, even though we didn’t know him very well yet. He was still pretty sleepy from his big day. Being born is exhausting!
