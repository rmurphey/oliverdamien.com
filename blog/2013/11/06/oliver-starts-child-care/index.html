Oliver Starts Child Care

I’m getting ready to head back to work, so Oliver is slowly transitioning into child care. We found a really great child care center that’s very close to my work, so I can drop him off on my way to work, pick him up on my way home from work, and even visit in the middle of the day if I want to.

Oliver and I toured the whole center a while back, and then we visited both infant rooms together with Rebecca, and then he and I visited his infant room and spent some time with his primary child care provider. Tomorrow, Oliver will go to child care all on his own in the morning, from around 9:30 to around 1:30, so he can start getting used to being there without me. We’ll do the same thing the following Tuesday, and then he’ll go full days on Tuesday and Wednesday the following week when I start back to work. The next week (Thanksgiving week), he’ll go full days on Monday, Tuesday, and Wednesday, which will be his regular child care schedule.

I really hope tomorrow goes well! I’m most worried about him learning to sleep at child care. At home, he’s still in his rock-n-play sleeper (an inclined rocking bassinet). At child care, he’ll be in a crib. At home, he wears a sleep sack and a swaddle strap for naps and nights, to keep his arms from flailing. At child care, he’ll just be in a sleep sack. I’m glad that his room has a separate alcove where all the cribs are, and the lights are turned low in that part of the room, and they have a sound machine going all the time. I hope those things make it a bit easier for him to learn how to sleep there.
