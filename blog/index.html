Our Boy Can Roll Over

Yep, it’s true. Our boy can roll over, front to back. It’s pretty amazing. Last night we had some friends over for dinner, and I decided to put him down on the floor for tummy time after I changed his diaper. I put him face down on the floor, with his arms bent underneath him like I normally do. He lifted his head waaay up, so he was looking straight at me where I was positioned in front of him on the floor. And then it happened. He pushed off with his right arm and wound up halfway between his side and his back (don’t ask me how he stalled out at precisely that position). After I praised him a ton, I yelled to Rebecca and our guests, “You guys have got to come in here!” They came and I told them what Oliver had done. I didn’t put him back on his tummy for some reason; I’m not sure why. I think I was afraid it was a fluke and he wouldn’t do it again.

Then this morning after a diaper change, I put him down for tummy time again. It was the same thing, only faster. He lifted his head waaay up, pushed off with his right arm, and he was on his back. I praised him a ton and put him back on his tummy. He did it again. Pretty amazing.

Then this afternoon after a diaper change, I decided to put him down again. He did the whole thing again, except pushed off with his left arm this time. I called Rebecca in so she could witness the awesomeness of our boy. He did it again, pushing off with his left arm again. She was impressed.

Next time I hope to get a video of it. But Rebecca says that maybe four times rolling over in one day is enough, so it likely will be tomorrow before I can videotape him. Bummer. I think I’m even more excited about this than Oliver.

Oliver’s Florida Grandparents Visit

This week, my parents — Oliver’s Florida grandparents — came for a visit. Oliver had a nice time getting lots of attention from them. He got a horsey ride and napped with Grandpa, and went on walks and cuddled with Grandma.

The timing of my parents’ visit was especially good because Rebecca got sick on Tuesday and couldn’t help me with Oliver much overnight for several nights. So I was very happy to see my Mom and Dad each morning!

My parents and I took Oliver on quite a few outings this week. We went to Babies R Us, Buy Buy Baby, Duke Gardens, Elmo’s, A Southern Season, and Locopops, and Dad went to Pelican’s Snoballs to get us some shaved ice. The weather was mostly nice this week, so we also went on a lot of neighborhood walks.

My parents also babysat Oliver on Friday night so Rebecca and I could go out to dinner. We got some quick burgers at Bull City Burger. It was nice for the two of us to have some time together — but we were definitely ready to get home to see Oliver too.

Oliver’s First Real Pediatrician Visit

Oliver had his first real pediatrician visit today! He was seen by the doctors in the hospital where he was born when he was just a few days old, but this was his first appointment with his actual pediatrician in North Carolina.

The pediatrician checked Oliver out, and said he seemed very healthy. She recommended that we do a newborn screening, because she predicted that it would be difficult to get the results of the newborn screening done in the hospital where Oliver was born. We said that was fine, so poor Oliver had to have his heel stuck and then a series of circles on a piece of paper filled in with his blood. Needless to say, he did not like that at all.

Jul 16th, 2013

The Long Trip Home

Today was a big day — the day we got to fly home to North Carolina with Oliver. Although we really looked forward to being home, we were worried that it would be a hard trip for Oliver. He’d be in unfamiliar surroundings, and taking off and landing might be uncomfortable for him. We needn’t have worried. He did great. He slept most of the time (including during takeoff and landing). He was so sleepy that we had to wake him for most of his feedings. I couldn’t get him to nurse very much at all, which was a little concerning, but I figured I’d work with him on that when we got to North Carolina.

Unfortunately, there’s no direct flight from Denver to Raleigh-Durham, so we needed to book our flight through Atlanta. Let’s just say that Atlanta is not Rebecca’s favorite airport. It is usually very crowded and busy, with insufficient seating at the gates. To make matters worse, our flight out of Atlanta was delayed by about an hour and a half. It turns out, though, that traveling with a newborn is a good conversation-starter. We were very popular people at our gate. Lots of people wanted to hear our story (and give us their opinions on various child-rearing tactics). We were impressed that no one tried to touch Oliver or asked to hold him.

The flight crew on the flight from Atlanta to Raleigh-Durham were very nice, and gave us “wings” (one for each of us and one for Oliver). They also gave us a bottle of wine to congratulate us on adopting Oliver.

We arrived around midnight, and our friend Julie picked us up in our car (so the carseat was all set). We crammed all the luggage into the car, and headed home. We tried to get to bed as quickly as possible, but we had to do a little unpacking to be sure we had all the baby gear we’d need for the night. We finally got to bed a little after 2:00 am. We were exhausted after our long day.

North Carolina Says We Can Come Home!

Today we learned that we got clearance from North Carolina to enter the state with Oliver! Rebecca immediately got online and booked plane tickets home for us tomorrow. The court hearing for the relinquishment of the birth parents’ rights is not until early next week, so we will be leaving the state “at risk” — if anything goes awry with rights relinquishment, we’ll need to return to Colorado with Oliver. But we think the chances of that are low, and we really want to be home with him as soon as possible.

We also got together with Oliver’s birth mom and her mom tonight. We ate dinner together, exchanged contact information, admired how great Oliver’s birth mom looks so soon after Oliver’s birth, and talked about how wonderful Oliver is. It was nice to spend that time together.

July 6, 2013

Today, we mostly hung out in the hotel room with Jan and Morgan, cuddling and spending time with Oliver. During tummy time today with Aunt Morgan, Oliver managed to turn his head from one side to the other. We were all very impressed!

Around mid-day, Rebecca and I both headed to the nearby pedestrian mall for some much-needed haircuts, and Jan and Morgan babysat Oliver. It was strange to be away from our baby, but good to know that Jan and Morgan were watching over him.

We all took a short outing to Buy Buy Baby in the afternoon. We picked up a few newborn sleeper outfits, and replaced our plastic bottles with glass bottles instead. Rebecca and I had both accidentally left nearly half of a feeding in the plastic bottles we had (which are opaque, making it hard to determine when Oliver had finished his milk). We didn’t want to do that again.

That night, we went out to dinner again. Melissa tried the Moby wrap — which Rebecca had really liked — and it didn’t work as well for her. So she put Oliver in the Ergo Baby Carrier (with the infant insert) instead. It worked much better for her, and we had another successful dinner out at a nice restaurant in the nearby pedestrian mall. Oliver peacefully slept the entire time.
